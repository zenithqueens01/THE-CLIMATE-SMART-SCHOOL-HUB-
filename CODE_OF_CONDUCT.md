# Contributor Code of Conduct

## 🌍 Our Pledge

We as contributors and maintainers of the Climate Smart School Hub 
(CSSH) pledge to make participation in our project and community 
a respectful, inclusive, and harassment-free experience for everyone — 
regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity, 
level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, 
or sexual identity.

We are united by a shared mission: protecting children and building 
climate-resilient communities across Africa and beyond.

---

## ✅ Our Standards

### Positive behavior includes:
- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Respecting different viewpoints and experiences
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- Focusing on what is best for the community and mission
- Showing empathy towards fellow contributors
- Celebrating diverse contributions from across the globe

### Unacceptable behavior includes:
- Harassment, trolling, or insulting comments
- Personal or political attacks
- Publishing others' private information without permission
- Discriminatory jokes or language
- Any conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate
  in a professional or community setting

---

## 👥 Our Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for:
- Clarifying the standards of acceptable behavior
- Taking fair and appropriate corrective action when needed
- Removing, editing, or rejecting contributions that violate this code
- Banning contributors who repeatedly violate community standards

---

## 🌐 Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces including:
- GitHub repository (issues, pull requests, comments)
- Project communication channels
- Public representation of the project at events or online

## ⚖️ Enforcement

Confirmed violations of this Code of Conduct may result in:

1. **Warning** — A private written warning with clarity on the violation
2. **Temporary Ban** — Temporary removal from the project community
3. **Permanent Ban** — Permanent removal for severe or repeated violations
